Full-Body Depth Extraction

The complete VP-8 depth signal from head to feet — frontal and dorsal images, 3D surfaces, anatomical profiles, and AI sculptural reconstructions.

Source Image

The Enrie 1931 full-length negative captures the entire Shroud: both the frontal (front of body) and dorsal (back of body) images arranged head-to-head in the center. We process each half independently through the same CLAHE + normalization pipeline used for the face studies.

Frontal Body

Anatomical Centerline Profile

Crossed Hands Position

The depth map clearly shows both hands crossed over the pelvis region, with the left hand over the right. This is visible as a bright horizontal band at approximately 45% of the body height. The hand position is consistent with first-century Jewish burial practice and matches descriptions from STURP researchers.

Dorsal Body

The dorsal image — the back of the body — is extracted from the right half of the full-length negative. The same depth pipeline is applied.

The low correlation (r=0.25) between frontal and dorsal profiles indicates they encode independent but complementary depth information. Where the frontal depth is high (face, chest), the dorsal is relatively low, and vice versa — consistent with a body lying on a flat surface with the cloth draped over it.

Full-Body Sculptural Reconstructions

The full-body depth map is resized to 384×768 portrait format and processed through ControlNet Depth (conditioning scale 0.95) with Stable Diffusion 1.5. Both original and healed (symmetrized) versions are generated.

Hand Position & Arm Geometry

The crossed hands are one of the most distinctive features of the Shroud body image. We extracted the hand region (45–55% of body height) and analyzed the depth profile for crossing geometry and thumb visibility.

MeasurementValue
Hand region location45.0–54.9% of body height
Crossing angle~20° from horizontal
Hand arrangementRight hand crossed over left
Thumb visibilityNot visible — consistent with post-mortem adduction

The absence of visible thumbs is a well-known feature of the Shroud and has been attributed to post-mortem retraction of the thumbs into the palms (cadaveric spasm) or intentional positioning. The depth data confirms this: no distinct thumb protrusion is detectable in the high-frequency detail of the hand region.

Height Estimation

Using the full-body depth map, we identified the crown of the head and the feet positions from the centerline intensity profile and calibrated against the known Shroud cloth length.

ParameterValue
Head position11.5% from top of image
Feet position92.4% from top of image
Body occupancy80.9% of image height
Estimated height1.76 m (5’9.4”)
Uncertainty range1.70–1.83 m (5’7”–6’0”)
Published estimates1.75–1.81 m (5’9”–5’11”)

Our estimate of 1.76 m is consistent with published Shroud research estimates (Giulio Fanti: ~1.75 m; Shroud Science Group: ~1.78 m). This height would place the individual in the upper range for first-century Palestinian males (average ~1.65–1.70 m), consistent with a notably tall person for that era.

Limitations

The full-body source image (2370×2321) is significantly lower resolution than the face close-ups used in Studies 1 and 2. The frontal body crop is only ~1125 pixels wide, yielding a 300-pixel-wide analysis grid. Facial detail is minimal at this scale. The full-body reconstructions should be understood as representing overall body posture and proportions, not fine anatomical detail. Height estimation carries ±3–4% uncertainty from calibration, cloth draping, and body position.
