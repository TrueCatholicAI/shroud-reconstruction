Key Question: Does Higher Resolution Reveal New Facial Features?

The data shows a mixed picture. Some measurements (nose height: ratio 0.97, nose to chin: ratio 0.94) remain relatively stable across resolutions, suggesting these features are reliably detected regardless of scale. However, many measurements show significant divergence:

  • Dramatic decreases: Forehead to nose peak (0.09×), nose peak to bridge (0.20×), depth nose to forehead (0.06×)
  • Dramatic increases: Mouth width (5.60×), face width at eyes (4.26×)
  • Depth measurements: Cheek depths remain stable (~1.16× ratio), but forehead depth increases significantly (2.13×)

This suggests that while higher resolution captures more detail, it may also introduce boundary detection artifacts that significantly affect linear measurements. The depth measurements appear more robust.

Interpretation: Convergence vs. Divergence

✓ Convergent (Stable) Measurements

Measurements with ratio near 1.0 are reliable regardless of resolution:

  • Left/Right Cheek Depth: Ratio ~1.16 — depth values stabilize at higher resolution
  • Nose Height: Ratio ~0.97 — nearly perfect scaling consistency
  • Nose to Chin: Ratio ~0.94 — reliable proportional measurement

These measurements represent features that are robustly detected across scales and likely reflect actual image properties rather than resolution artifacts.

✗ Divergent (Resolution-Dependent) Measurements

Measurements with ratios far from 1.0 show significant resolution sensitivity:

  • Face Width at Eyes: Ratio 4.26 — dramatically increases at high resolution
  • Mouth Width: Ratio 5.60 — most divergent measurement
  • Forehead to Nose Peak: Ratio 0.09 — dramatically decreases
  • Depth Nose to Forehead: Ratio 0.06 — extreme divergence

These measurements are sensitive to edge detection algorithms and may reflect interpolation effects or boundary detection changes at different resolutions.

Limitations

  • Resolution-dependent boundary detection: Edge detection algorithms may identify different boundaries at different resolutions, leading to the observed divergence in linear measurements.
  • Interpolation artifacts: Upscaling from the original image to different resolutions may introduce interpolation artifacts that affect feature detection.
  • No ground truth available: Without known reference measurements, it is impossible to determine which resolution provides the most accurate representation.
  • Limited resolution range: The analysis uses only three resolution levels (150×150, 300×300, 500×500), which may not capture the full convergence behavior.
  • Region-specific artifacts: Some facial regions (forehead, mouth) show more resolution sensitivity than others, suggesting localized image quality issues.
  • Depth measurement limitations: While depth measurements appear more stable, they are derived from the same underlying image data and may share similar limitations.
  • Algorithm dependency: Results are specific to the Miller projection algorithm used; other algorithms may yield different resolution sensitivities.
  • Image quality: The underlying image quality of the Shroud photograph may limit the effective resolution beyond which additional detail is noise rather than signal.

Conclusion

The high-resolution Miller analysis reveals that facial feature detection shows mixed stability across resolutions. Depth measurements (particularly cheek depth, chin depth) demonstrate reasonable convergence, suggesting these represent reliable features. However, many linear measurements show significant divergence, with some changing by factors of 4-6× between low and high resolution.

Higher resolution does not uniformly improve feature detection. Some features (mouth width, face width) show dramatic increases at 500×500, while others (forehead region) show dramatic decreases. This suggests that resolution selection should be guided by the specific features of interest, and that multi-resolution analysis provides a more complete picture than any single resolution.

Recommendation: For reliability analysis, focus on stable measurements (cheek depth, nose to chin). For exploratory analysis of edge features, note that high resolution may reveal different boundaries than low resolution.
