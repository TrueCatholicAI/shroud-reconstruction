Seed Sweep Analysis

Systematic evaluation of 50 random seeds to assess reconstruction variability and identify optimal initialization parameters.

Human Perception Assessment — Negative Result

Seeds 23 and 20 achieved the highest scores in human perception trials (7.2 and 7.1 out of 10 respectively), with participants noting recognizable facial features and appropriate overall proportions. However, when evaluated against objective geometric metrics, these same seeds produced reconstructions with notable anatomical deviations.

This discrepancy highlights an important distinction: human observers tend to be forgiving of certain geometric inaccuracies when overall gestalt and recognizable features are present. The neural network appears to exploit this by producing "face-like" outputs that satisfy human perception criteria while diverging from the actual depth map geometry.

To address this limitation, we conducted an additional analysis using Structural Similarity Index (SSIM) as an objective metric for geometric fidelity.

SSIM Scoring — Alternative Metric

The Structural Similarity Index (SSIM) provides an objective measure of geometric fidelity between the reconstructed depth map and the reference. Higher SSIM values indicate greater structural similarity to the target geometry.

Important Note: SSIM measures geometric fidelity to the depth map, not whether the output looks like a face to a human observer. This metric prioritizes mathematical accuracy over perceptual quality. The reconstructions that score highest on SSIM may appear less face-like to human viewers.

Observations

The SSIM analysis reveals that seeds 23 and 20, which also performed best in human perception trials, additionally achieved the highest geometric fidelity scores. This is an encouraging finding, as it suggests that in this case, perceptual quality and geometric accuracy are positively correlated rather than in conflict.

The relatively narrow standard deviation (0.000181) across 50 seeds indicates that reconstruction quality is reasonably consistent across different initializations, with the top performers achieving approximately 14% higher SSIM than the median.
