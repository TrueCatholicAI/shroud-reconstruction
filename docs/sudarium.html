Sudarium of Oviedo Analysis

Geometric and statistical comparison with the Shroud of Turin body image

Overview

The Sudarium of Oviedo is a bloodstained cloth traditionally associated with the Passion of Christ. Unlike the Shroud, it shows no dorsal body image. However, the bloodstain patterns may encode positional information about the face that can be compared against the Shroud's frontal image.

This analysis uses ROI-based geometric comparison to evaluate whether specific facial features in the Sudarium bloodstains align with corresponding regions in the Shroud depth map.

Methodology

ROI-Based Comparison

Regions of Interest (ROIs) were defined around key facial landmarks: forehead, eyes, nose, mouth, and chin. The ROI radius was set to 8 pixels. Overlap scores were computed by comparing Sudarium feature positions against expected positions derived from the Shroud depth map.

Permutation Testing

To assess statistical significance, random permutations were applied to the Sudarium positions:

  • X offset range: -50 to +50 pixels
  • Y offset range: -50 to +50 pixels
  • Rotation range: -180° to +180°
  • Number of permutations: 1,000
  • Random seed: 42

Limitations

  • Feature Detection Variability: The Sudarium bloodstain patterns are diffuse and may not precisely localize facial features. Automated feature detection may miss subtle but meaningful correlations.
  • Single Landmark ROI: Using an 8-pixel radius ROI around single points captures limited neighborhood information. Larger-scale pattern matching might reveal additional correlations.
  • Binary Threshold Sensitivity: The choice of threshold for depth map binarization affects the overlap score. A systematic threshold sweep could provide more robust results.
  • Geometric Constraints: The cloth may have shifted between the body being covered by the Sudarium and later placement on the Shroud, introducing geometric distortions not accounted for in this analysis.
  • Bloodstain vs Body Image: The Sudarium contains blood, not the scorch/impression mechanism proposed for the Shroud body image. The comparison assumes positional correspondence regardless of formation mechanism.

Null Hypothesis Test

To rigorously assess whether the observed overlap scores could have occurred by chance, we conducted a permutation-based hypothesis test. The null hypothesis (H₀) states that the Sudarium facial features have no specific spatial relationship to the Shroud body image—any observed overlap is due to random alignment.

Test Parameters

  • Number of permutations: 1,000
  • X offset range: -50 to +50 pixels
  • Y offset range: -50 to +50 pixels
  • Rotation range: -180° to +180°
  • Random seed: 42

Statistical Interpretation

Result: No Statistically Significant Correlation Detected

All p-values are above the conventional significance threshold of 0.05. The observed overlap is not statistically distinguishable from what would be expected by chance alignment.

Specifically:

  • The left eye region shows the lowest p-value (0.368), but this is far above the 0.05 threshold. An overlap score of 31.715 falls within the expected range of random variation (mean: 20.348, std: 21.499).
  • All other regions (forehead, right eye, nose, mouth, chin) have p-values of 1.0, indicating their observed overlaps are entirely consistent with random chance.
  • The overall p-value of 0.1965 confirms that the combined analysis does not reject the null hypothesis.

Conclusion: Under the conditions tested (1,000 permutations with the specified offset and rotation ranges), there is no evidence of a non-random spatial relationship between Sudarium bloodstain positions and Shroud body image features.

Caveats

This analysis has important limitations:

  • Multiple Comparisons: Testing 6 regions simultaneously increases the risk of Type I error (false positives). Even if any individual p-value were below 0.05, correction for multiple comparisons would be required.
  • Limited Permutations: With only 1,000 permutations, the minimum achievable p-value is 0.001. More permutations would provide finer resolution.
  • Parameter Bounds: The offset and rotation ranges limit the tested transformations. Optimal alignment might fall outside these bounds.
  • Feature Detection: The analysis depends on accurate automated detection of bloodstain features. Manual landmarking might yield different results.

Conclusions

The ROI-based geometric comparison between the Sudarium bloodstain positions and the Shroud body image does not reveal statistically significant spatial correlation under the tested conditions. The null hypothesis of random alignment cannot be rejected.

This result does not necessarily contradict traditional associations between the two cloths. It simply indicates that the specific geometric test performed here— using automated ROI overlap scoring—is not sensitive enough to detect any relationship, or no such relationship exists in the tested form.

Further investigation might consider:

  • Manual landmarking of bloodstain features
  • Larger ROI radii to capture neighborhood patterns
  • Cross-correlation methods instead of binary overlap
  • Broader rotation and offset search ranges
