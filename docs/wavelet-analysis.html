Multi-Frequency Depth Decomposition

Wavelet-Based Signal Separation for Forensic Analysis

Overview

Wavelet decomposition provides a mathematically rigorous approach to separating the facial signal from cloth-related noise artifacts. Unlike simple Gaussian smoothing, which indiscriminately blurs all spatial frequencies, wavelet analysis allows us to isolate specific frequency components and quantify their contribution to the overall depth information.

Using a Symlet-4 wavelet with 4 levels of decomposition, we can separate the depth map into distinct frequency bands and analyze each component independently. This technique reveals that the facial features are concentrated in specific spatial frequency ranges, while cloth texture artifacts occupy a different frequency domain.

Wavelet decomposition separates the depth map into multiple frequency components

Frequency Band Analysis

The depth map decomposition reveals three distinct frequency bands with remarkably different energy distributions. Each band corresponds to different structural features in the image:

Contains the overall head contour and major body features. Represents the fundamental shape information that defines the subject's silhouette.

Mid-Frequency Details

Anatomical Features

Contains critical anatomical details: nose bridge, brow ridges, eye sockets, lip contours. This is the forensic-critical information for identification.

Fine surface texture including the linen weave pattern and measurement/processing noise. These artifacts are not body-derived.

Signal Quality Metrics

Quantitative analysis of the decomposed signal reveals excellent separation between the facial information and cloth artifacts:

Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) ~25 dB
Face Signal Energy 99.7%
Cloth Artifact Energy 0.3%
Face/Cloth Ratio 333:1

The SNR of approximately 25 dB indicates that the facial signal is well-separated from cloth artifacts. A ratio of 333:1 between face signal energy and cloth noise confirms that the depth information is overwhelmingly body-derived.

Signal quality analysis showing clean separation between facial features and cloth artifacts

Implications for Image Formation

The multi-frequency analysis provides critical constraints on the possible image formation mechanisms for the Shroud of Turin:

  • Low Spatial Frequency Constraint: The face signal is concentrated in low spatial frequencies, with gross facial structure captured in approximately 5-10 cycles across the face width. This suggests the formation mechanism could not have produced detail finer than these spatial scales.
  • Cloth Weave Contribution: The linen weave contributes less than 0.3% of total depth information. This confirms the signal is body-derived, not cloth-derived, ruling out mechanisms that would imprint weave texture onto the body image.
  • Formation Resolution Limit: The ~0.7% mid-frequency content containing anatomical details suggests a formation resolution of approximately 7-14 pixels across the face width, constraining any proposed formation mechanism to produce detail at this scale.
  • Selective Detail Preservation: The hierarchical energy distribution (99% → 0.7% → 0.3%) suggests a formation mechanism that preferentially produced low-frequency body contour information while preserving some mid-frequency anatomical features and suppressing cloth texture.
